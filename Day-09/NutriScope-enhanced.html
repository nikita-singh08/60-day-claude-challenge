Nutrition sources & disclaimer

Where the numbers come from, and the limits of what this tool can tell you.

Data sources
01
USDA FoodData Central
Reference values for globally common foods, nuts, seeds, dairy, and packaged staples.
02
Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT) 2017 — National Institute of Nutrition, ICMR
Primary reference for home-style Indian dishes and preparations (roti, dal, poha, idli, dosa, and similar).
03
ICMR-NIN Recommended Dietary Allowances (RDA), 2020
Basis for the daily micronutrient targets (iron, calcium, vitamin C, vitamin D, B12, zinc, magnesium, potassium, vitamin A, folate).
04
Mifflin-St Jeor equation
Basis for the BMR calculation used to derive your energy and macro targets.
Full disclaimer
NutriScope is a self-contained educational and planning tool. All nutrient values are per-100g approximate averages for common preparations — actual content varies with brand, ripeness, cut, cooking method, and portion size. Calculated targets (BMR, TDEE, macros, and micronutrient RDAs) are population-level estimates, not personalized medical guidance, and do not account for individual conditions such as pregnancy, chronic disease, medication interactions, or diagnosed deficiencies.

Nothing in the Dashboard, Risk Analysis, Meal Planner, or Recommendations tabs constitutes medical, dietetic, or clinical advice, and none of it should be used to diagnose or treat any condition. If you have ongoing symptoms, a diagnosed condition, or are making significant dietary changes, please consult a registered dietitian, nutritionist, or physician.
